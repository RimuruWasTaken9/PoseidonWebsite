Crushed sesame seeds with sugar grounded to paste, flavored with almonds, pistachios, or chocolate.

Dolmatis

Vine leaves stuffed with rice.

Vasilopita

Only sold for Christmas & New Year's.

Round bread with sesame seeds on top spelling out the next year. The tradition is that there is a coin in the bread, a family cuts out one piece each from oldest to youngest, with the winner having good luck for the year.

Christopsoumos

Only sold for Christmas & New Year's.

Similar to the Vasilopita, round bread with sesame seeds on top without the coin.

Kouloura

Only sold for Easter & Greek Easter

Braided round bread with dyed red eggs in the middle with a cross over the eggs.

Tsioureki

Only sold during the holidays

A long braided version of the holiday breads.

About company

The Poseidon Bakery, founded in 1923, began supplying New York with authentic treats from ancient Greece.

Today, the Poseidon Bakery is one of New York's authentic family run businesses. Generations have passed, and the neighborhood has changed, but the delicacies at the bakery have remained the same.
